# Copilot Isn't Leaking Your Data. Your Permissions Are.

Microsoft Copilot isn't leaking your data.

Your permissions are. Copilot just made it easier to find.

Here's what nobody tells you before rollout:

## The Numbers That Should Scare You

- 3 million sensitive records accessed by Copilot per organization on average (2025 data)
- 15% of business-critical files at risk from oversharing
- 67% of enterprise security teams concerned about AI exposing sensitive data
- US Congress banned staff from using Copilot over data security concerns

→ Copilot doesn't break your security. It exposes what was already broken.

## What Copilot Actually Does

- Inherits every permission your users already have
- Searches across SharePoint, Teams, OneDrive, Outlook
- Surfaces files that were "hidden" in nested folders
- Makes finding sensitive data trivially easy

→ That salary spreadsheet buried 5 folders deep? Copilot finds it in seconds.

## The Real Problem: Permission Sprawl

- SharePoint sites set to "public" by default
- Inherited permissions nobody audited in years
- "Everyone" and "Everyone except external users" groups
- Link sharing with no expiration

→ Most orgs don't know what's exposed until Copilot shows them.

## What You Must Do Before Rollout

- Audit SharePoint and OneDrive permissions (not optional)
- Kill "Everyone" access on sensitive sites
- Implement sensitivity labels in Microsoft Purview
- Review sharing links - especially "Anyone with the link"
- Test with a pilot group BEFORE enterprise rollout

→ The time to fix permissions is BEFORE you give AI access to everything.

## The Bottom Line

Copilot is a permission audit tool disguised as a productivity feature. If you're not ready for what it exposes, you're not ready for Copilot.
