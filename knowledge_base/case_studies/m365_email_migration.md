# Case Study: Cross-Platform Email & Collaboration Migration to Microsoft 365

**Role:** Chris Wetzel — architected and executed these migrations and managed the full lifecycle.

**Migrations:** Numerous diverse client organizations, from legacy on-premises Exchange Servers and Google Apps (Workspace) → Microsoft 365
**Outcome:** Zero email downtime or message loss; corporate communications standardized on a secure, scalable cloud platform

---

## The Problem

Diverse client organizations were running legacy on-premises Exchange Servers or Google Apps (Workspace) — fragmented, aging platforms that were harder to secure, scale, and support. They needed to move to a modern Microsoft 365 ecosystem without losing email or disrupting global users in the middle of doing business.

---

## The Migration Lifecycle (Zero Downtime)

Managed the entire migration lifecycle, engineered so email never went dark and no messages were lost in transit:

- **Pre-flight domain validation** — confirm ownership and readiness before any cutover
- **MX record cutovers** — timed and sequenced to route mail without loss
- **Data staging** — mail and data in place before the switch, so cutover is a flip, not a copy

The hard requirement throughout: **zero email downtime or message loss during transit.**

---

## Securing the Tenant (Part of the Migration, Not After)

Each migration included hardening the destination M365 tenant — security configured as part of the cutover, not a separate follow-up project:

- **Identity protection**
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)**
- **Data loss prevention (DLP)** parameters

---

## Seamless Global Cutover

Delivered seamless cutovers for global end-users across:

- **Outlook clients**
- **Mobile devices**
- **Shared resources**

Standardizing corporate communications on a secure, scalable cloud infrastructure.

---

## Why It Worked

- **Zero-downtime discipline** — pre-flight domain validation + careful MX timing + data staging = no dark mail and no lost messages.
- **Secure by cutover** — identity protection, MFA, and DLP configured during the migration, not bolted on later.
- **Cross-platform** — handled both Exchange and Google Workspace sources into one standardized M365 target.
